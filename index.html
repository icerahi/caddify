Part A

The Interview Script

Context: You are interviewing a Senior Project Manager at Data Soft.

Question 1: Can you tell us about the company's size?

"My company name is Data Soft. It is a large software company. In total, we have over 400 employees. However, I manage a specific divisionâ€”the 'FinTech Squad'â€”which has about 35 developers and testers."

Question 2 & 3: What sector and industry are you in?

"We are in the Private Sector. The industry is IT and Software Development. We build software for banks and government projects."

Question 4: How would you define your organization? What is the main business?

"I define us as a 'Digital Solution Provider.' Our main business is custom software. Clients come to us with a problem, and we write the code to solve it."

Question 5: What are the main attributes? Which is most important?

"The main attributes are Innovation, Precision, and Teamwork. The most important is Teamwork. Writing code is like building a house; if the backend developer and the frontend developer don't talk, the app crashes."

Question 6: What is the organizational structure? Is it responsive?

"We use a Matrix Structure. It means employees have two bosses: they report to me for the project, but they report to a 'Tech Lead' for their coding standards. Is it responsive? Yes. We work in 'Sprints' (2-week cycles). If the client changes their mind, we can adjust in the next Sprint."

Question 7: What is the main role of management?

"My main role is Unblocking. Functions: I plan the schedule, I talk to the clients so my developers don't have to, and I remove obstacles. Challenge: The biggest challenge is 'Deadlines.' In software, things always break, but the client still wants the product on Monday."

Question 8: Who are the formal and informal leaders?

"The Formal Leader is the Director or CTO. The Informal Leader is usually the 'Senior Architect.' He creates the toughest code. When he speaks, everyone listens because he is a genius at problem-solving."

Question 9: Describe the culture.

"The culture is 'Cool but Intense.' Traditions: We have 'Stand-up Meetings' every morning where we stand and talk for 15 minutes. Environment: We have a gaming room and coffee, but when a deadline is coming, people work late nights."

Question 10: What about the environment?

"It is purely mental work. External Forces: Technology changes fast. A coding language we used 3 years ago might be old now. We have to study constantly. Internal Forces: Bugs. Fixing errors in the code is the biggest daily stress."

Question 11: What management methods do you use?

"We use Social-Psychological and Economic methods. Social: We use the 'Agile' method. This empowers the team to make their own decisions. Economic: We have 'Project Completion Bonuses.' If we finish the app on time, the whole team gets a bonus."

Question 12: What is the dominant leadership style?

"It is Democratic (Participative). You cannot force a programmer to code. You have to ask them, 'How long will this take?' or 'What is the best way to build this?' Interaction: We solve problems together on a whiteboard. Advantage: We get better solutions because 10 brains are better than 1. Limitation: Meetings. Sometimes we spend too much time talking and not enough time coding."

Question 13: How does this style influence performance?

"It makes the team feel ownership. They aren't just typing; they are building their product. This makes the quality much higher."
