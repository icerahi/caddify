MD IMRAN HASAN

0:00 - 6:00 Intro, Datasoft Structure

"Good morning, everyone. We are Group PI24E. My name is MD IMRAN HASAN, and today, along with my colleagues Augustine and Emmanuel, we will present our project on Management and Leadership."

"We conducted an exclusive interview with Mr. Mustafiz, a squad leader at Datasoft, one of the largest software companies in Bangladesh. We will compare his Democratic Management Style with the Visionary Leadership of Sam Altman from OpenAI."

Part A: The Interview Context

"First, let's look at Datasoft. Datasoft is a massive IT firm with over 400 employees. However, management doesn't happen at the 400-person level."

"Mr. Mustafiz manages a specific unit called the 'FinTech Squad.' This is a team of 35 developers and testers building software for banks. He defined the company as a 'Digital Solution Provider.' Clients come with a problem, and his team writes the code to solve it."

Organizational Structure

"We asked him about the structure. He explained that Datasoft uses a Matrix Structure. This means employees technically have two bosses: they report to Mustafiz for the project timeline, but they report to a Tech Lead for code quality."

"Is it responsive? Yes. They work in 'Sprints'—short 2-week cycles. If a client changes their mind, the team can pivot instantly in the next sprint."

The Role of Management

"This was my favorite part of the interview. When we asked Mr. Mustafiz about his main role, he didn't say 'Commanding.' He said his job is 'Unblocking.'"

"He plans the schedule and talks to the clients so that his developers don't have to. He removes obstacles."

"His biggest challenge? Deadlines. As he told us: 'Things always break in software, but the client still wants the product on Monday.'"

TRANSITION: "Now, Augustine will take you inside their office culture."

2

AUGUSTINE CHINEMELU

6:00 - 12:00 Culture, Methods, Style

"Thank you, Imran. My name is Augustine. Let's talk about what it feels like to work at Datasoft."

Culture & Environment

"Mr. Mustafiz described the culture in three words: 'Cool but Intense.'"

  • The 'Cool' part: They have a gaming room and unlimited coffee.
  • The 'Intense' part: When a deadline is coming, they work late nights to fix bugs.

"Traditions: They have a 'Stand-up Meeting' every single morning. For 15 minutes, everyone stands up to give quick updates. It keeps the energy high."

"He mentioned that the work is purely mental.
Internal Force: Bug fixing. This is their biggest daily stress.
External Force: Technology changes fast. A language used 3 years ago might be old now, so they must study constantly."

Management Methods

"To manage this intelligent team, they use:
1. Social-Psychological Methods: They use Agile. This empowers the team to make their own decisions.
2. Economic Methods: They use Project Completion Bonuses. If the app is finished on time, the whole squad gets a financial bonus."

Dominant Leadership Style

"The dominant style is clearly Democratic."

"Mr. Mustafiz said something very important: 'You cannot force a programmer to code.' You have to ask them, 'What is the best way to build this?'"

"How they interact: They solve problems together on a whiteboard.
The Advantage: As he said, '10 brains are better than 1.' You get better quality solutions.
The Limitation: Meetings take time. Too much talking means less time for coding."

TRANSITION: "Now, Emmanuel will introduce our Global Leader comparison."

3

EMMANUEL KUFRE

12:00 - 18:00 Sam Altman & Comparison

"Thank you, Augustine. I am Emmanuel. We have seen the collaborative, democratic world of Datasoft. Now, let’s look at the Visionary world of Sam Altman, CEO of OpenAI."

Part B: Sam Altman

"While Mustafiz focuses on finishing a project on time, Sam Altman focuses on changing human history. He represents Visionary and Transformational Leadership. His goal is AGI (Artificial General Intelligence)."

"The Difference in Power:
Mr. Mustafiz has Expert Power—his team listens to him because he helps them.
Sam Altman has Referent Power—his team listens because they believe in his vision."

"When Sam was fired last year, 95% of his staff threatened to quit. They were loyal to the Mission, not just the job."

Part C: Comparison

"Let’s compare them side-by-side:"

Datasoft (Mustafiz)

  • • Style: Democratic
  • • Goal: Working Product
  • • Advantage: Stability

OpenAI (Sam Altman)

  • • Style: Visionary
  • • Goal: Disruption
  • • Advantage: Rapid Growth

Our Preference

"So, which is better? Our group agreed: We prefer the Democratic Style of Datasoft."

"Mr. Mustafiz told us that his style makes the team feel 'Ownership.' They aren't just typing; they are building their product. We value that collaboration over the high-stress volatility of a Visionary leader."

Conclusion

"To conclude: If you want to build a house, you need a Democratic team where the backend and frontend talk to each other. If you want to go to Mars, you need a Visionary."

"Context is everything."

"Thank you for listening to Group PI24E. We are happy to take your questions."
